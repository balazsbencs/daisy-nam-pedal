Live interface

Try the pedal.

Turn a knob with drag, mouse wheel, or arrow keys. Click the Gain encoder to open presets; press and hold it for 800 ms while browsing to edit. Tap or hold the footswitches exactly as you would on the device.

What can I do here?

    Interface demonstration only—no audio is processed. Press T while focused in the pedal to simulate holding both footswitches.

    01 · First sound

    Quick start.

    1. Connect the guitar to the input and your amplifier, powered monitor, or audio interface to the output using the analog I/O circuit in your build.
    2. Turn down the downstream system before powering the pedal. Set Gain near 1.0 and Volume low.
    3. Power the Daisy-based pedal and wait for the performance screen. The first stored preset loads automatically.
    4. Bring up downstream volume slowly, then use the five encoders for the live level and EQ balance.
    5. Tap the right footswitch for the next preset or the left footswitch for the previous preset.
    Hardware boundaryThe firmware repository defines digital controls and display wiring, but not a complete analog I/O or power circuit. Follow the circuit used by your build; do not connect instrument or amplifier signals directly to GPIO. See the hardware guide.
    02 · Audio path

    Signal flow.

    INPUT→GAIN→GATE→COMP→NAM→IR→EQ→DELAY→VOLUME

    The normal path is mono at 48 kHz and is copied to both outputs. Bypass sends the dry input directly to both outputs. Tuner mode analyzes the dry input and mutes both outputs. Gate, compressor, and delay values are stored in presets; the current device UI does not expose their parameters for live editing.

    04 · Performance

    Play and make live changes.

    The main screen shows the preset, amp model, cabinet IR, active/bypass state, and five live values. Turning any encoder marks the preset EDITED. These changes affect sound immediately but remain temporary until saved.

    • Save: hold right / FS1 for 800 ms. The edited marker clears after QSPI programming completes.
    • Revert: hold left / FS2 for 800 ms. The stored preset is reapplied and the edited marker clears.
    • Change preset: tap either footswitch. Unsaved live changes are discarded when another preset loads.
    05 · Browse

    Select a preset directly.

    1. Click the Gain encoder from performance mode.
    2. Turn it to move the bounded cursor through the preset list.
    3. Click it to load the highlighted preset, or tap either footswitch to leave without changing presets.
    4. To edit the highlighted preset instead, hold the Gain encoder for 800 ms.
    06 · Edit

    Change the stored rig.

    The editor exposes eight fields: Model, Cab, Input Gain, Output Volume, Bypass, Bass Frequency, Mid Frequency, and Treble Frequency.

    1. Turn the Gain encoder to highlight a field.
    2. Click it to enter value-edit mode. The highlight changes color.
    3. Turn to change the value; click again to return to field navigation.
    4. Tap right / FS1 to apply the edited preset and save it to its QSPI slot.
    5. Tap left / FS2 to discard the working copy and return to performance mode.

    EQ gain values edited in performance mode are preserved when the editor saves. A synthesized preset created because no preset records exist has no QSPI slot, so it cannot be persisted from the device.

    07 · Tuner

    Tune silently.

    Hold both footswitches together for 800 ms from performance mode. The pedal analyzes the dry input, skips the amp/effects path, and mutes both outputs. The screen shows note, octave, and cents offset when a stable signal is detected. Hold both switches again to return.

    Individual footswitch taps are suppressed during the chord, so entering or leaving tuner mode does not also change presets.

    08 · Display

    Read the status at a glance.

    • ACTIVE / BYPASS: whether DSP or clean passthrough is selected by the preset.
    • EDITED: live values differ from the stored preset.
    • AMP / CAB: resolved model and IR names; Off means no IR.
    • Five meters: Gain, Bass, Mid, Treble, and Volume settings—not audio level meters.
    • AUDIO OVERLOAD: the audio callback approached its 1 ms deadline; choose a supported/lighter data set before relying on the rig live.
    09 · Recovery

    Troubleshooting.

    SymptomCheckAction
    No outputTuner active, Volume at 0, downstream muted, or analog path incomplete.Exit tuner, raise Volume carefully, test downstream gear, then verify the build's analog circuit.
    Dry sound onlyBYPASS badge or unsupported/missing model.Load an active preset. Rebuild the data image with a supported A2 Nano model if model loading failed.
    No cabinet effectCAB shows Off or IR failed to load.Select a preset with an IR and verify the data image contains that exact IR name.
    Edits disappearPreset changed before saving, or preset is synthesized without a flash entry.Hold FS1 to save before changing presets; add named preset entries to the data image for persistence.
