01 · Boundary

Before you start.

This repository defines the digital user interface around a Daisy Seed: five encoders, two footswitches, an ST7789 display, USB, QSPI data storage, and firmware behavior. Those connections are complete below.

This is not yet a complete pedal hardware packageThe repository does not contain a verified analog guitar input/output circuit, power-supply schematic, bill of materials, PCB, enclosure drawing, or mechanical drilling template. You cannot finish a reproducible, stage-ready pedal assembly from this repository alone. Supply those reviewed design inputs before connecting audio jacks or external power.

Use the labels printed on your exact board and compare its orientation with the official Daisy Seed product documentation. The code uses libDaisy's seed::D0–seed::D30 names; physical header numbering is not interchangeable with those names.

04 · ST7789

Display signal wiring.

The driver targets a 240×320 ST7789 in portrait orientation. It uses SPI1 master mode 3 (CPOL high, second-edge sampling), transmit-only, 8-bit words, prescaler 4. There is no MISO connection.

Display boards are not pin-compatible by label aloneSome ST7789 modules include a regulator or backlight transistor; others expose the panel directly. Confirm VCC and BLK requirements before power. A matching controller name does not make the power pins interchangeable.
05 · Rotary controls

Wire passive quadrature encoders.

For each encoder, connect its common terminal to system ground and its A/B terminals to the assigned D pins. The firmware enables internal pull-ups; the passive contacts pull each phase low. Do not feed an external voltage into A, B, or click.

  • Gain / Encoder 1: A→D0, B→D1, push contact→D2; both the phase common and push-button return go to ground.
  • Bass / Encoder 2: A→D7, B→D8, common→ground.
  • Mid / Encoder 3: A→D9, B→D10, common→ground.
  • Treble / Encoder 4: A→D27, B→D28, common→ground.
  • Volume / Encoder 5: A→D29, B→D30, common→ground.

If a knob counts backward, power down and exchange only its A and B wires. Leave the common terminal on ground. The primary encoder uses libDaisy debounce in the main loop; encoders 2–5 are sampled from the audio callback and accumulated atomically.

06 · Switches

Wire momentary, normally-open footswitches.

Connect one terminal of FS1 to D15 and one terminal of FS2 to D16. Connect the other terminal of each switch to system ground. Internal pull-ups keep released inputs high; pressing closes the contact to ground.

SwitchSignalReturnFirmware gesture
Right · FS1D15 / PC0GroundTap next/apply; hold 800 ms save
Left · FS2D16 / PA3GroundTap previous/cancel; hold 800 ms revert
BothD15 and D16 lowGroundHold together 800 ms to toggle tuner

Use momentary contacts, not latching switches. The chord logic suppresses trailing individual release events after both switches have been held.

08 · Sequence

Assemble in testable stages.

  1. Freeze the missing design inputs. Select/review the analog I/O circuit, power architecture, exact display module, BOM, PCB/perfboard layout, and enclosure drawing.
  2. Mark board orientation. Label D0/D1 ends and ground/power rails before placing the Seed. Photograph the bare placement for later fault tracing.
  3. Build power and analog sections separately. Validate them against their own schematics before attaching the Daisy or guitar equipment.
  4. Wire common ground. Daisy, passive controls, display logic, and the reviewed analog circuit need the intended shared reference. Follow the analog design's grounding topology rather than daisy-chaining high-current returns through switch wiring.
  5. Wire and test the display. Verify its connector pin order with a meter/datasheet; connect only the signals listed above.
  6. Wire Encoder 1 and both footswitches. These provide navigation and mode access. Continuity-test each contact before adding the remaining encoders.
  7. Wire Encoders 2–5. Keep A/B pairs identifiable; do not add unused click wires.
  8. Inspect, continuity-test, then power through a current-limited setup defined by the reviewed power design.
  9. Bring up digital functions before audio. Use USB logs, display, and control checks first; attach the analog path only after those pass.
09 · Stop before smoke

Pre-power checklist.

  • Confirm the exact Daisy Seed revision/orientation and every D-number against board labels.
  • Confirm the power design's input voltage, polarity, regulator output, current limit, and ground with no Daisy installed.
  • Measure no unintended continuity between each power rail and ground.
  • Continuity-test every GPIO wire end-to-end and verify no adjacent-pin shorts.
  • Released footswitches and encoder contacts are open to ground; pressed/closed contacts approach zero ohms to ground.
  • Display VCC and BLK wiring match the exact module datasheet; BLK is not drawing LED current from D24.
  • SCK/MOSI/CS/DC/RST/BLK match D22/D18/D13/D14/D26/D24 in that order.
  • No analog signal, jack contact, or external voltage is connected directly to a Daisy GPIO.
  • Loose wire strands, metal enclosure contact, and unsupported boards cannot short when a footswitch is pressed.

If any check is ambiguous, stop. A firmware pin table cannot resolve an unknown module or power circuit.

10 · First power

Bring up one subsystem at a time.

  1. Daisy + USB only: use the approved power arrangement, connect USB, and confirm the board enumerates. Flash the QSPI bootloader/application if needed.
  2. Serial log: confirm NamPlatform booting..., board version, storage status, model/preset counts, controls, display, then audio start.
  3. Display: confirm a portrait performance screen. A lit blank panel points to SPI/control wiring; no backlight points to the module-specific power/BLK path.
  4. Controls: rotate each knob and watch only its field. Tap each switch, then test save/revert holds and the both-switch tuner chord.
  5. Data: flash a verified image at 0x90200000; confirm the serial entry count and displayed model/IR names.
  6. Audio last: connect the separately verified analog I/O path to a low-level test setup, start with downstream gain reduced, then confirm bypass before active DSP.
